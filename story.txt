It started, like most bad decisions do, with someone saying, “Let’s just get one ice cream and share.”
Vanya was the one who said it, blissfully unaware as to how cynical the other 2 were. Gauri didn’t trust “sharing” as a concept, Akshath was already calculating how uneven the distribution would be, and the ice cream cart guy looked like he’d seen friendships end over less. Still, five minutes later, the three of them were standing under a half-broken umbrella, holding a single, dangerously top heavy triple scoop cone that was already beginning to melt in the Bangalore heat, with each of them pretending they were totally fine with the arrangement, and each of them absolutely not meaning it.
A drop of melted chocolate slipped onto Vanya’s wrist, and she wiped it away like nothing was happening.
Gauri leaned in just enough to remind them both she was watching the portions closely.
Akshath adjusted his grip on the cone, steadying it with an almost unnecessary level of precision.
For a moment, they stood there in silence, negotiating more with their expressions than their words.
In the end, they passed the cone around carefully, each taking just enough to keep things even, as if that had been the plan all along.
